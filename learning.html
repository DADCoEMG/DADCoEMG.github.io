Learning

What is EMG?

EMG stands for "Electromyography", a biodiagonstic technique for recording electrical activity produced by muscles. A clenched muscle emits a very particular type of signal that can be observed by small metal electrodes. This data can then be used for a multitude of different tests and even to accomplish certain digital tasks. While currently only useful for medical and research purposes, DADCo seeks to utilize process in order to control consumer electronics. Check out Colin's blog for cool EMG experiments!

Why EMG?

When EMG electrodes are touching your skin, a lot of data can be captured. This data can detect the motion of muscles, and with some calculation can detect what gestures or movements you are making. This means that with EMG technology, all devices can be gesture-controlled simply by wearing these electrodes.

Our Products

DADCo's flagship product will be an EMG-integrated smartwatch. Early prototypes have shown some very positive results including the movement of robotic hands and the playing/pausing of music applications. We will explore the multitude of different ways that EMG could be used with modern electronics, and hopefully help a few people along the way!

Our Friends
