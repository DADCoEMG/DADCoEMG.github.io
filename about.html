Dan Berg

Daniel Berg has extensive experience with solid modeling and 3D Computer-Aided Design, including 6 months working with Wendt Corp in Buffalo, NY. During this time, he was responsible for designing components for recycling processing systems as well as generating solutions for issues that arise during the manufacturing, and installation processes. Mr. Berg’s skills and experience will be very valuable in creating attractive and marketable products to the public. Mr. Berg will be leading ergonomics design as well as working on the hardware team. Currently enrolled in a paramedic course, Mr. Berg is expecting to gain valuable knowledge in human anatomy and physiology that will prove useful in product development. In addition Mr. Berg will work in cooperation with Mr. Fausnaught filling business and administrative roles.

Tre DiPassio

Joseph DiPassio has a strong background in electrical engineering, physics, and mathematics including extensive study in signal processing. During a summer internship in Silicon Valley, Mr. DiPassio developed a passion for entrepreneurship and the start-up atmosphere as well as a desire to create something that would both improve people’s lives and be desireable to consumers. Mr. DiPassio brings knowledge of electrical systems and engineering efficiency to the hardware team of DADCo. Mr. DiPassio will be working on the hardware-software bridge as well as designing the control systems that will allow the biofeedback electrodes to communicate with our technology.

Colin Fausnaught

Colin Fausnaught worked for OpenBCI, a brain-computer interface company, from May 2016 to December 2016. In addition Mr. Fausnaught has lead an OpenBCI project team through RIT’s Society of Software Engineers for the last 3 years. During his time at OpenBCI he has fostered a deeper understanding of how biofeedback systems work and would like to commercialize what he has learned. With nearly 6 years of programming experience Mr. Fausnaught is an experienced programmer and leader. Mr. Fausnaught will be invaluable in managing software development practices within the company and leading software development teams. Mr. Fausnaught created the first prototype EMG watch that DADCo will be using and is hoping to change the world with this exciting new technology.

John Grosh

John Grosh has been programming for nine years across various platforms and languages. He has large amounts of experience working with open-source and team projects as a developer and collaborator on several publicly-available libraries. He worked with Rochester Software Associates for 7 months on software development and scripting. He is passionate about runtime efficiency and well-designed user interaction. Mr. Grosh’s interest in machine learning and algorithmic efficiency will be crucial to the success of DADCo’s EMG signal processing systems.
